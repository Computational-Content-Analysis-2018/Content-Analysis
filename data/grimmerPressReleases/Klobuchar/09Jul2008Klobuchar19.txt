                                                                                                            Senator Klobuchar Votes to Pass Medicare Bill for Seniors  Military Families by Veto Proof Majority                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              Supports measure that contains quality improvement incentives                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    July 9  2008 Washington  D C   U S   voted today to preserve access to quality health care for Americas Medicare beneficiaries by avoiding a harmful cut in physician payments and creating incentives to reward quality improvement practices    The Medicare Improvements for Patients and Providers Act passed the Senate today by a vote of 69 30 and will now go to the President  Senators stood and applauded shortly before the roll call as Senator Ted Kennedy  D MA   returned to the chamber for the vote for the first time since being diagnosed with a brain tumor   Thousands of Minnesotans count on Medicare for quality health services and the peace of mind that comes with dependable medical care  Klobuchar said  It is fitting that we could join today in this important vote with Senator Kennedy  who was present at the creation of Medicare and has championed quality health care through his long and distinguished career    The measure blocks a 10 6 percent cut in payments that was scheduled to take effect for Medicare physicians  a cut that would have caused many doctors to stop seeing or taking new Medicare patients  according to health care analysts    The bill also contains incentives to improve health care quality in Medicare  It will reward doctors who report on quality improvement measurements and provide incentives for doctors and clinics to make greater use of electronic prescribing technology to improve medical accuracy   Its time that Medicare rewarded the kind of high quality  low cost medicine that our doctors and hospitals practice in Minnesota  so this is an important first step toward reform  Klobuchar said
