                                                                                                            Klobuchar to South Korean Ambassador  Keep Markets Open to American Beef                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Minnesota s Farmers and Ranchers Produce  1 Billion Worth of Cattle and Calves Each Year   100 Million of That is Exported to Other Countries                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    January 17  2007  Washington  D C   U S   today advocated for Minnesota farmers and ranchers  telling South Korean Ambassador Lee Tae sik that it is crucial to the Minnesota economy for his country to re open its market to imported American beef  Klobuchar participated in a meeting with the Ambassador as a member of the Senate Committee on Agriculture  Nutrition and Forestry  where she explained that Minnesota farmers and ranchers produce one billion dollars worth of cattle and calves each year  About 100 million dollars of that is destined for the export markets  I emphasized that  after more than three years  it is long overdue for Korea to resume imports of American beef products  said Klobuchar  South Koreas continued restrictions unfairly penalize farmers and ranchers in Minnesota  who have gone to great lengths to ensure that American beef continues to be the safest and healthiest in the world  Beginning in December 2003  when a case of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy  mad cow disease  was discovered in the U S   South Korea banned the import of all American beef  Prior to that ban  South Korea was the third largest export market for American beef  with annual imports valued at more than  800 million  Last September  South Korea lifted its ban on boneless beef from cattle under 30 months of age  However  it continues to ban all U S  bone in beef and boneless beef from cattle over 30 months  South Korea has also turned away two beef shipments that contained any bone fragments
