                                                                                                            Klobuchar Helps Minnesota Youth Prepare for the Future Through Early Childhood Education                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         Klobuchar backs bipartisan bill to reauthorize successful Head Start program                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     November 14  2007 Washington  D C   U S   today voted in support of the Head Start for School Readiness Act of 2007  which authorizes over  22 billion to help give children around the nation the help they need to succeed in school and beyond  The bill passed the Senate today with overwhelming support today by a vote of 95 0  For forty years Head Start has proven that it works  that education is the key to the future  and that quality early childhood education pays off  said Klobuchar  This will ensure we invest early in children  and give them every opportunity to learn  develop  and grow into productive and engaged citizens  Each year  approximately 15 000 Minnesota families benefit from the Minnesota Head Start program  The legislation passed today will likely increase that number substantially by making the program more accessible  allowing families that make under  26 800  or 130 percent below the federal poverty level  to enroll in Head Start  The bill will also provide additional funding for Minnesotas Head Start program  which currently receives approximately  83 million annually in federal funding  in addition to over  19 million in state funding  The bill authorizes over  22 billion for Head Start over three years  and requires that 40 percent of the funds be set aside to improve program quality  including increasing salaries for Head Start teachers  The bill passed in the House of Representatives today by a vote of 381 36 and now goes to the President desk for final passage
