                                                                                                            Senator Klobuchar Votes to Override President s Veto of Medicare                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Klobuchar fights for nation s seniors to ensure continued access to quality care                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 July 15  2008 Washington  D C   U S   tonight voted to override the Presidents veto of the Medicare Improvements for Patients and Providers Act  The bill will preserve access to quality health care for Americas Medicare beneficiaries by avoiding a harmful cut in physician payments and improving incentives to reward quality improvement practices     The bill passed the Senate last week by a vote of 69 30 after Senator Ted Kennedy  D MA  returned to the Senate chamber to vote for the first time since being diagnosed with a brain tumor  The President vetoed the legislation today  but the House and Senate acted immediately to overturn the veto to make it law  The override passed the Senate by a vote of 70 26    Thousands of Minnesotans count on Medicare for quality health services and the peace of mind that comes with dependable medical care  Klobuchar said  I cast my vote to override the Presidents veto tonight because this legislation is vital for the millions of patients who rely on this program to receive quality health care    The measure blocks a 10 6 percent cut in payments that was scheduled to take effect for Medicare physicians  a cut that would have caused many doctors to stop seeing or taking new Medicare patients  according to health care analysts    The bill also contains incentives to improve health care quality in Medicare  It will reward doctors who report on quality improvement measurements and provide incentives for doctors and clinics to make greater use of electronic prescribing technology to improve medical accuracy   Its time that Medicare rewarded the kind of high quality  low cost medicine that our doctors and hospitals practice in Minnesota  so this is a first step toward reform  Klobuchar said
