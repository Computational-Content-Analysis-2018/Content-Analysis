                                                                                                            Senator Klobuchar Backs Bipartisan Agreement to Raise LIHEAP funding                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             House spending package has  2 57 billion for heating aid program December 17  2007 Washington  D C   U S   announced today that the Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program  LIHEAP  would receive  2 57 billion in fiscal 2008 under an Omnibus Appropriations agreement reached this week by House Democrats after Klobuchar and 49 other Senators signed a letter to Congressional appropriators requesting extra funds for the program  That sum is nearly  1 billion higher than the funding proposed by the President and is designed to address the rapidly rising costs of homing heating fuels this winter  With a cold winter already setting in for Minnesota homeowners  Im proud that we have an agreement to secure the additional funding that LIHEAP needs  Klobuchar said Monday  Klobuchar worked with fellow members of the Minnesota delegation to secure the higher LIHEAP funding  Federal funding for LIHEAP has increased by 10 percent during the last four years  but the number of households receiving LIHEAP aid has increased by 26 percent  Natural gas and home heating oil prices are expected to rise by 30 percent to 50 percent this year compared to 2006  The Omnibus Appropriations bill was expected to pass the House Monday night and head to the Senate for a vote by the end of this week
