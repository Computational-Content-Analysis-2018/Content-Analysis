                                                                                                            Klobuchar Continues Push to Crack Down on Energy Market Speculation and High Energy Costs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        Takes to Senate floor to highlight how to provide consumers with short term relief and bold long term energy strategy                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            June 11  2008 Washington  D C   U S   took to the Senate floor today to continue her push to bring consumers relief at the gas pump by cracking down on speculation in world energy markets  while also developing long term renewable energy sources to reduce Americas dependence on foreign oil  Klobuchar emphasized the importance of taking action to help consumers who are being hit hard as gas tops  4 a gallon   People all across Minnesota are feeling the squeeze of unprecedented gas prices  Klobuchar said  If you drive past a Costco store in the Twin Cities suburbs where they are selling gas at discount prices  you will see gas lines that will remind you of the OPEC oil embargo of the 1970s    Yesterday Klobuchar supported the Consumer First Energy Act of 2008 which would have helped reduce energy prices  crack down on speculators  and help developed a long term energy strategy  Klobuchar has been working aggressively to help consumers deal with the rising price of gas and investigate speculation in the energy markets that have driven gas prices to all time records    Audio
