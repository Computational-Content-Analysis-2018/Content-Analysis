         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  Washington  DC  Today  Senator Edward M  Kennedy announced that the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee will begin a series of hearings and meetings on the reauthorization of the No Child Left Behind Act next week  The Act became law in 2002 and is due for reauthorization this year  On Thursday  February 8  2007 at 10 00 AM  the Committee will host a roundtable discussion on school improvement strategies  Senator Kennedy issued the following statement  The reauthorization of the No Child Left Behind Act is a top priority for the American people  The Senate will make necessary changes to improve and strengthen the Act  and ensure its effectiveness for students  parents  and teachers  In the HELP Committee  we will hold hearings  roundtables  and other events to hear from experts  educational leaders  and others who know whats working in classrooms and what isnt  I look forward to discussing these issues and moving forward with reauthorization in Congress on a bipartisan basis Participating in the roundtable discussion will be those on the front lines in our schools  working on the state  district and school levels to improve school performance Over the next several months  the Senate education committee will meet to discuss    High schools and dropout prevention strategies   Teacher quality   Accountability and AYP   Assessment issues   Supplemental services and choice   Students with disabilities and NCLB   English Language Learners and NCLB  and  Strategies for parental involvement and community supports for schools     Laura Capps Melissa Wagoner   202  224 2633
