         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  WASHINGTON  D C  Senator Edward M  Kennedy  Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  sent the following letter to associations representing colleges and universities today  informing them of the committees findings with respect to its investigation of the student loan industry  The Senator urges the institutions to assist in efforts to ensure that the higher education financing system is free of conflicts of interest  The letter and a list of recipients are below May 4  2007Dear  insert name  The current controversy over student loans demonstrates the importance of clear and enforceable standards for those who administer them  I write to report on some of the problems that our committees investigations have uncovered  Given the coordinating role of the  XXXXXX  and its relationship with presidents and chancellors across the country  I urge you to share this information with institutions to help ensure that we finally see an end to these problems Of particular concern to the committee are the number of ways in which lenders have tried to influence college officials in order to be placed on the colleges preferred lender list  The disturbing practices include  College officials serving on lenders boards of directors and advisory boards  Ownership of lenders stock by college officials who make loan decisions for the college  College officials receiving gifts from lenders  including trips  meals and entertainment  Lenders providing company staff to assist schools in processing loan applications  Colleges allowing lenders to put the colleges name  emblem or logo on its loan products Decisions about financing the cost of higher education are enormously difficult for young people  especially as costs increase from year to year  A students debt load can influence courses of study  career choices  and many other personal decisions  Given these high stakes  I believe every college should avoid conflicts of interest with lenders  Every official to whom students turn for advice should be an unbiased representative of the students interests  with no financial stake in lenders I hope this information will be helpful in establishing guidelines for all colleges and universities to maintain the integrity of the student loan process With respect and appreciation for all you and your members do to advance the cause of U S  higher educationparticularly the needs of students  Sincerely  Edward M  Kennedy ChairmanHigher education association presidents receiving this letter  Rev
