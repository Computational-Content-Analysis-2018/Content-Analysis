         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE  WASHINGTON  D CToday  Senator Edward M  Kennedy  Chairman of the Senate Health  Education  Labor and Pensions Committee  released the following statement in response to the passage very early this morning by the United States Senate  by a vote of 78 18  of the Higher Education Access Act of 2007  The bill  sponsored by Senators Kennedy and Enzi  will provide more than  17 billion to help students and families pay for college  Senator Kennedy said  The passage of the Higher Education Access Act tonight was a victory not only for students and their families  but for the American people  With this new congress we made education a national priority again  and weve given the next generation the tools they need to compete in the global economy  Not since the GI bill  has education been made such a priority in the nations budget  Some of the most deserving students will attend college because of the Pell grant increase  The struggling middle class will see relief from debt with flexible repayment options and public service loan forgiveness  Today  the doors of opportunity were opened for millions of hardworking students and an investment was made in their futures      Laura Capps  Melissa Wagoner  202  224 2633
