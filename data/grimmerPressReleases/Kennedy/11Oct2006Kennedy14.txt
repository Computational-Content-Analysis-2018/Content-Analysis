         FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE    Washington  DC  Today  Senator Edward M  Kennedy issued a statement in response to a new public health report that estimates that 600 000 civilians have died in Iraq from the war  The study  conducted by the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health  details the rise in violent deaths since 2003 based on surveys with Iraqi families  According to the study  about 15 000 deaths occur each month  Below is the statement from Senator Kennedy   This new study is a chilling and somber reminder of the unacceptably high human cost of this war  standing in stark contrast to the administration s refusal to admit that Iraq is spiraling into a civil war  The conclusions are clear  the war in Iraq has resulted in increased violence and suffering and we must not stay on the same failed course any longer     Laura Capps Melissa Wagoner  202  224 2633
