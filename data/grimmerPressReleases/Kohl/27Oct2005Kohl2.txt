KOHL  OBEY ANNOUNCE  1 75 MILLION FOR WISCONSIN S EFFORT TO FIGHT CHRONIC WASTING DISEASE                     Press Release      For Immediate Release    October 27  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL  OBEY ANNOUNCE  1 75 MILLION FOR WISCONSIN S EFFORT TO FIGHT CHRONIC WASTING DISEASE  Funding included in FY2006 Agriculture Appropriations Bill    WASHINGTON U S    Senator Herb Kohl and Congressman David Obey announced today that they have  secured  1 750 000 in funding for Wisconsin s Chronic Wasting Disease  CWD  eradication efforts as part of the FY2006 Agriculture  Rural Development and Related Agencies Appropriations which passed the Senate House conference committee last evening  Kohl the ranking member of the Agriculture Appropriations Subcommittee  and Obey the senior Democrat on the House Appropriations Committee identified CWD eradication as a priority for funding     Chronic Wasting Disease continues to threaten Wisconsin s deer population  These federal funds will help offset some of the costs involved in preventing more infected deer and will help in using technology to keep CWD in check   Said Kohl    Wisconsin s efforts to contain and eradicate CWD are very important for both public safety and our outdoor tradition   Obey added    Hopefully  these new funds will help the people on the front lines tackle the CWD outbreak    CWD continues to plague deer herds and the Wisconsin funding will be used to move herd owners toward radio frequency identification systems  offset sampling costs  and assist with depopulation expenses   The funding is part of  18 71 million included in the bill for national CWD eradication efforts    The conference report on the FY06 Agriculture Appropriations bill will now go before the full House and Senate for final ratification and then will be sent to the President to be signed into law
