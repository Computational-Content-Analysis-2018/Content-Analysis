KOHL DIRECTS  200 000 TO JUNEAU COUNTY BUSINESS INCUBATOR PROJECT                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    July 19  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL DIRECTS  200 000 TO JUNEAU COUNTY BUSINESS INCUBATOR PROJECT     WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that the Senate appropriations bill that funds the Department of Housing and Urban Development includes  200 000 for the Juneau County Economic Development Corporation s  JCEDC  proposed small business incubator in the Village of Necedah   Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified the project as a priority for funding as part of the FY2006 federal budget   AJob creation is central to a region s economic viability and the community s prosperity   This is an ambitious project to help Juneau County compete in a changing marketplace  and I m pleased that we were able to bring federal support to the initiative   Kohl said  The JCEDC has presented a business incubator design to address Juneau County s high unemployment rate  The design focuses on the use of an existing four hundred thousand square foot industrial building located south of the Village of Necedah  At its core  the facility will have a small business incubator and a home for Juneau County s Investors and Entrepreneurs Club  The objective is to provide a setting in which the entrepreneur can obtain support for creating a business  while challenged employees learn the skills necessary to be productive members of the labor force  Using this setting  JCEDC projects that eight to ten new businesses will graduate from the facility per year   The FY2006 funding bill for the Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  was passed by the Senate Subcommittee on Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary and HUD today and is expected to be approved by the full Appropriations Committee on Thursday
