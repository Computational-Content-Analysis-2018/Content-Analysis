FEINGOLD ANNOUNCE ACTIVATION OF WISCONSIN FEDERAL NOMINATING COMMISSION  Merit selection panel will recommend candidates for U S  Attorney in Western District     WASHINGTON  DC    U S  Senators Herb Kohl and Russ Feingold today announced the activation of an 11 member federal nominating commission responsible for making recommendations for the U S  Attorney vacancy in the Western District of Wisconsin  Members of the Commission  which has been in existence in Wisconsin since 1979  serve two year terms   The Commission helps the Senators fulfill their Constitutional duty to advise the President on federal nominations   The Commission was last used in 2003 to fill the Federal Judgeship vacancy on United States Court of Appeals for the Seventh Circuit    The fair and independent advice we receive from the commission is a valuable part of our vetting process for federal nominations   I am grateful for their service and I look forward to hearing the recommendations of the men and women on this panel   Kohl said    Once again  the commission will screen and recommend candidates for an important position in our federal system of justice   Feingold said     I am proud of this tradition that has greatly contributed over nearly three decades to public confidence in Wisconsin s federal judges and prosecutors    The commission charter details the number of members each Senator may appoint based on the political party of the Senators and the President   Kohl nominated Christine Bremer of Wausau and Brent Smith of La Crosse to the commission  Feingold nominated Sheriff Ann Hraychuck of Polk County and Ronald Sklansky of Madison   The commission is chaired by the Dean of the University of Wisconsin law school   The commission is rounded out by four nominees named by Congressman F  James Sensenbrenner and two members chosen by the State Bar of Wisconsin   Use of a federal nominating commission to recommend choices for vacancies in the federal judicial system dates back to 1979  when it was instituted by Wisconsin Senators Gaylord Nelson and William Proxmire
