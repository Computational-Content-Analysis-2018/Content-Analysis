KOHL SECURES  281 000 FOR KENOSHA HARBOR RETAINING WALL PROTECTION                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    Novemeber 14  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES  281 000 FOR KENOSHA HARBOR RETAINING WALL PROTECTION     WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that he secured  281 000 in federal funding for reconstruction and emergency protection of the Kenosha Harbor retaining wall  The funding is included in the FY 2006 Energy and Water Appropriations conference report which was passed by the Senate today  Last year Kohl also secured  50 000 for the project as part of the federal budget   The Kenosha area relies on heavily on commercial and recreational navigation   said Kohl   Without this necessary maintenance  the continual deterioration of the retaining wall could lead to other problems and am confident these funds will be put to good use   The project is located at Kenosha Harbor on the west shore of Lake Michigan in the City of Kenosha  Wisconsin   A wood retaining wall  constructed in 1916  adjacent to the Simmons Island Marina  is failing due to age and fluctuating lake levels   This wall provides protection and access to the harbor and marina   This harbor is one of the few deep water storm and emergency ports along Lake Michigan
