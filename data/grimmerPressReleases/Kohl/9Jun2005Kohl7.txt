KOHL SECURES  350 000 FOR MILWAUKEE URBAN FORESTRY PROGRAM TO STOP STORMWATER RUNOFF   IMPROVE PLAYGROUNDS                     Press Release      For Immediate Release    June 9  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES  350 000 FOR MILWAUKEE URBAN FORESTRY PROGRAM TO STOP STORMWATER RUNOFF   IMPROVE PLAYGROUNDS      WASHINGTON  D C    U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that he secured  350 000 for the City of Milwaukee to replace asphalt playgrounds with trees to stop stormwater runoff and improve school grounds  The funding is included in the FY 2006 Interior and Related Agencies Appropriations bill  which the Senate Appropriations Committee approved this afternoon  The project will be part of the Urban and Community Forestry Program  Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified Milwaukee s project as a priority for funding   Projects like this that serve a dual purpose are good investments of our scarce federal dollars  Urban forestry programs have been used with great success in other communities  and I m pleased that the City of Milwaukee will benefit from this funding   Kohl said  The City of Milwaukee has embarked on an ambitious plan to stop stormwater runoff and improve school playgrounds  The plan involves planting 1 000 trees at 75 school playgrounds around the city that are currently covered in asphalt  This not only will improve water quality in the watershed  it will also improve school grounds  All told the project will plant a total of 3 000 trees around the city  The FY 2006 Interior Appropriations Bill will next be considered by the full Senate
