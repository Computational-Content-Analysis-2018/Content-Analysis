KOHL SECURES FUNDING FOR WISCONSIN PROJECTS IN FY2007 AGRICULTURE APPROPRIATIONS BILL                     Press Release      For Immediate Release    June 22  2006    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES FUNDING FOR WISCONSIN PROJECTS IN FY2007 AGRICULTURE APPROPRIATIONS BILL  Bill includes funding for research  nutrition  dairy programs    WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that the FY2007 Agriculture Appropriations bill includes funding for a range of Wisconsin agricultural research  management and conservation projects  Kohl is the leading Democrat on the Senate Agriculture Appropriations Subcommittee   The bill provides over  100 billion in funding for the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  and a host of agriculture  nutrition and food safety programs                Wisconsin is home to an innovative agriculture community that is leading the nation in some bold programs and cutting edge experiments   said Kohl   This funding helps highlight the important research and development projects taking place in Wisconsin and I am pleased to work to see that they get the federal backing that they earn  compete for and deserve   Among the provisions designed to help dairy and other segments of Wisconsin agriculture  the bill includes   13 737 000 for Dairy Forage Research    These funds will be used in Madison  Marshfield and Prairie du Suc to enhance dairy research and modernize facilities   Plans call for improvements to animal digestion areas  laboratories  offices  and supporting infrastructure   The 700 animal facility in Prairie du Sac conducts research on systems for enhancing sustainable and competitive dairy systems that protect the environment  promote animal health and insure a safe and healthy food supply   10 000 000 nationwide to fight Johne s Disease   Funding to combat Johne s Disease  a contagious bacterial disorder that dramatically reduces milk production in infected herds   Wisconsin s work in this area has been augmented by federal funding ranging from  1 million to  1 7 million annually in recent years     2 893 400 for the Cereal Crop Research Unit  CCRU    Located in Madison  the CCRU develops better barley for US malting and brewing industries and more nutritious barley and oats for consumers nationwide   As the only public laboratory providing quality analysis to US barley researchers and breeders  CCRU plays a critical role in developing improved germplasm to meet the needs of America s barley industry   It utilizes an interdisciplinary team of scientists with facilities for basic and applied research and is the result of a unique collaboration between the Agriculture Research Service  ARS   the malting and brewing industries  and UW Madison     2 million for Animal Identification and Tracking   Premise registration and animal identification continue to be a priority for American agriculture and Wisconsin   Wisconsin s prototype premise registration system has served as a model in recent years as the nation recognizes the importance of reliable animal agriculture information     594 000 for the Babcock Institute at UW   The Babcock Institute links the US dairy industry to dairy farmers and processors around the world through educational programs and technology transfer to strengthen the dairy industries of developing countries as potential clients  It also aims to increase international commercial and scientific collaboration to enhance responsiveness to overseas markets   295 000 for Dairy Industry Revitalization   Through the UW Extension service  these funds provide support and mentoring to beginning dairy farmers  dairy modernization summits  design and management options for low cost retrofit milking parlors and related activities  803 503 for a Great Lakes Aquaculture Center    Though the Great Lakes region is home to 29  of the US population  the lakes generate less than 4  of seafood consumed in the region   The funds will allow the Great Lakes Aquaculture Center at UW Milwaukee to add additional scientific capacity to help alter that equation by identifying and eliminating constraints to aquaculture production     675 000 for Midwest Poultry Consortium   The Midwest Poultry Consortium supports and maintains poultry research programs at land grant institutions in 13 states  Wisconsin  Iowa  Colorado  Illinois  Indiana  Kansas  Michigan  Minnesota  Missouri  Nebraska  North Dakota  Ohio  South Dakota  and Florida    Research has emphasized avian diseases  animal welfare  and environmental waste management issues    396 000 for Potato Pest Management   Funds are used to reduce reliance on high risk pesticides and help create new markets for potatoes under integrated pest management practices   As a direct result of this funding  Wisconsin potato growers are developing niche markets for potatoes  and soon other commodities  that are grown in a sustainable manner   2 018 200 to improve potato genetics and storage  Valuable research has been performed in Wisconsin to reveal the mechanisms of genetic resistance to offset potential potato yield loss caused by pathogens   Additional research will be conducted on pesticide residue  plant physiology  fumigation and cultural practices to reduce storage losses  Kohl also secured funding to protect natural resources in Wisconsin and across the nation   941 000 for the Grazing Lands Conservation Initiative    The Grazing Lands Conservation Initiative in Wisconsin supports important technical assistance and competitive grants for demonstrations and applied on farm research   250 0000 for an Environmental Compliance Cooperative Agreement   These funds will be used in conjunction with the Dairy Business Association to help Wisconsin dairymen comply with facility siting requirements and help with smart growth planning  waste and odor management   16  296 000   for the Sustainable Agriculture Research and Extension program B This funding supports sustainable agriculture nationwide with  12 276 million for research and  4 026 million for extension activities to help farmers who want to implement more sustainable practices   1  188 000 for a nitrogen removal study and pilot program   Conducted by the Sand County Foundation in Wisconsin  develops market driven strategies to reduce the nitrogen runoff and other agricultural lands that degrade the nation s water supply   1 075 000 for Conservation Technology Transfer activities   Wisconsin s comprehensive conservation technology transfer program is composed of three integrated elements  The Pioneer Farm at the University of Wisconsin    Platteville provides research and educational programming about economic and environmental impacts of grazing  and cropping management for beef  dairy and swine production as well as whole farm runoff  and nutrient management  A cooperative agreement for technology transfer helps bring this work to the county level by providing grants which support county based educational programs  The Wisconsin Discovery Farm system provides essential  on the ground demonstration and educational resources to showcase conservation technology at different locations under different topographical conditions across the state    594 000 for Cranberry Conservation Activities in Wisconsin and Massachusetts  the nation s top cranberry producing states   Efficient and sustainable cranberry production relies on accurate management of water resources   These funds help cranberry growers manage water resources and address water quality issues   260 000 for Driftless Area Initiative   The Driftless Area is a unique region in the Upper Mississippi River Basin that encompasses southwest parts of Wisconsin  southeast Minnesota  northeast Iowa and northwest Illinois   Bypassed by the glaciers that covered much of the Midwest  this region has a diverse but fragile ecosystem  Land use is significantly changing in the region and these funds will be used to identify and encourage economically viable farming operations and practices and to protect streams and rivers   119 000 for the Wisconsin Land and Conservation Internships   This funding supports the next generation of conservationists by providing college students with valuable field experience working with natural resource professionals and landowners   Internships vary from county to county depending on local needs   Many interns help landowners with programs such as CREP and other wildlife related efforts   1 7 million for Geographic Information Systems   Administered through the University of Wisconsin  these funds provide mapping and planning resources for farmland preservation  emergency preparedness  growth management  land records modernization and other purposes through cooperative arrangements with  Penn State  University of North Dakota  University of Arkansas  Central Washington University and others  Geographic Information is also used for emergency planning and risk mitigation   257 000 for Grazing Research at the University of Wisconsin Madison    This research supports grazing based dairy production as an alternative to the more conventional systems based on harvested forage moved to confined dairy animals   500 394 for Michael Fields Agricultural Institute    This collaborative research is aimed at providing better corn for sustainable farming operations  It involves the USDA ARS Midwest Area  Corn Insect and Crop Genetics Research Unit  of Iowa  and the Practical Farmers of Iowa  PFI    The objective is to increase the nutritional value and adaptability to conditions where more nitrogen is provided through rotations and manure     50 787 million for the Resource Conservation and Development Councils   These councils  locally organized throughout the country  help plan and carry out activities that increase conservation of natural resources and support economic development in rural communities   The Administration s budget proposed substantial reductions for the RC councils across the nation   1 750 000 for Wisconsin to combat chronic wasting disease   Chronic Wasting Disease continues to plague deer herds and the Wisconsin funding will be used to move herd owners toward radio frequency identification systems  offset sampling costs and assist with depopulation efforts  Kohl also announced several provisions to nurture local markets and support economic growth   2 5 million to support growth in Wisconsin s value added dairy businesses    Product development  packaging and market placement assistance are among the most critical needs of emerging processors who are working to diversify and modernize Wisconsin s dairy sector   This project also promotes dairy modernization among dairy farmers    In addition to the federal and state resources involved  this effort enjoys considerable pro bono support of recognized dairy industry experts   Response has far exceeded expectations  with more than 100 emerging businesses utilizing this expertise to date and more coming forward each month   56 557 million to support state meat inspection programs   The bill continues full federal support for state meat inspection programs nationwide   Kohl has cosponsored separate legislation to allow interstate shipment of state inspected meat and poultry products   Wisconsin entrepreneurs point out that such authority will help create new business opportunities for local meat processors  Wisconsin has more state inspected facilities than any other state   809 000 for Urban Horticulture   This UW Extension initiative provides educational resources for greenhouse  landscaping  fruit and vegetable farming and community based gardening in urban suburban areas   Programs include business management and organization  marketing  intensive production systems  pest diagnostics  integrated pest management  and personnel management   248 000 for Agricultural and Rural Entrepreneurship Education  UW Madison    This funding supports extension programs to help value  added agriculture producers explore new markets  develop strategic plans  create farmers market and online business opportunities and perform realistic assessment and decision making by rural and farm families regarding these alternatives   188 000 for Rural Business enhancement at UW Platteville    This funding supports a center at Platteville that will identify strategic  high growth industry clusters  and develop an action plans to help them succeed     240 000 for Quantitative Analysis of Alternative Milk Policies   This research grant for UW Madison and University of Missouri supports timely and independent analysis of dairy policy changes  including trade related matters     545 000 for UW Madison s Food Systems Research Group   This group provides economic research related to the behavior of firms and markets in the U S  food system  It assesses the impact of government policies and regulations    particularly competition and trade policies    on the organization and performance of the U S  food system  Kohl also announced provisions to help families and youth   989 000 to help local school districts implement breakfast programs   Adequate nutrition  including breakfast  improves students  prospects for academic success   Grants under this program help Wisconsin schools implement innovative breakfast programs across the state   In school years 2005 and 2006 these grants provided start up funding at 63 sites  reaching 24 300 students  and helped 126 schools  that implement programs to enhance participation in school breakfast  reaching 60 700 students    4 563 million for the Agr Ability program B This program provides education and assistance nationwide to more than 800 farmers with disabilities  The goal of the project is to assist farmers with disabilities to farm successfully   Easter Seals of Wisconsin is a partner of the program   1 98 million for grants to youth organizations   This funding provides nationwide support for Girl Scouts  Boy Scouts 4 H and FFA for their ongoing work in rural areas   The Agriculture Appropriations bill now goes before the Senate for approval
