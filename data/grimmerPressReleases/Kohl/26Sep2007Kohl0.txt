KOHL BACKS EFFORT TO EXTEND  DO NOT CALL  PROGRAM INDEFINITELY                     Press Release      For Immediate Release    September 26  2007    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL BACKS EFFORT TO EXTEND  DO NOT CALL  PROGRAM INDEFINITELY      WASHINGTON   Today  U S  Senator Herb Kohl introduced a measure to strengthen and extend the  Do Not Call  initiative  a popular Federal Trade Commission  FTC  program to block telemarketing calls  Currently  telephone numbers registered with  Do Not Call  are only stored in the program s database for five years    after that consumers must  re register  their telephone number with the FTC to continue in the program  The Do Not Call Improvement Act of 2007  authored by Sen  Byron Dorgan  D ND  and sponsored by a bipartisan group of senators  would eliminate the five year expiration and keep numbers in the database forever                Do Not Call is a simple and efficient government initiative that has worked better than any of us could have expected when we passed it in 2002   Kohl said   My number is in the registry and telemarketers no longer call me four or five times a night  That s exactly what we wanted  so why should we have to sign up again  This bill says we don t have to go through that rigmarole twice               Since 2002  Kohl has fought for the  Do Not Call  registry   As chairman of the Judiciary Subcommittee on Antitrust and as a member of the Appropriations Commerce  Justice  State Subcommittee  now Commerce  Justice  Science Subcommittee   Kohl worked with then Federal Trade Chairman Timothy Muris to enact the FTC s national  Do Not Call  registry and won funding authorization for the FTC  speeding the program s implementation              According to the FTC  nearly 150 million households have registered telephone numbers with their anti telemarketing program
