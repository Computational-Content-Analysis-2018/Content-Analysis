KOHL SECURES  230 000 FOR GREEN BAY WATERFRONT REVITALIZATION                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    October 21  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES  230 000 FOR GREEN BAY WATERFRONT REVITALIZATION     WASHINGTON  D C   Today U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that the Senate appropriations bill that funds the Department of Housing and Urban Development includes  230 000 for construction to revitalize the Green Bay waterfront  Late last evening the Senate passed the Treasury  Transportation  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill by a vote of 93 to 1  Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified this project as a priority for funding    These funds will help Green Bay move forward in its plans to revitalize its waterfront   said Sen  Kohl   The development of the shoreline and the relocation of the coal dock will promote job growth and will act as a catalyst for economic development     The appropriation from the federal government will help fund the move of the C  Reiss Coal Dock from its current prime downtown waterfront location  opening up 40 acres for development  The coal dock has been a viable economic concern for over 100 years in downtown Green Bay  and for over the past forty years  C  Reiss and the city have been working on relocating the facility  both opening up the waterfront property for development and giving C  Reiss a more competitive position on the Fox River  The Senate Treasury  Transportation  Housing and Urban Development appropriations bill now goes to a joint Senate House conference committee for approval before it can be signed into law by the President
