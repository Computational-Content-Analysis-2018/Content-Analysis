KOHL SECURES  230 000 FOR GREEN BAY WATERFRONT REVITALIZATION                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    July 19  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES  230 000 FOR GREEN BAY WATERFRONT REVITALIZATION     WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that the Senate appropriations bill that funds the Department of Housing and Urban Development includes  230 000 for construction revitalization of the Green bay waterfront  Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified this project as a priority for funding   Waterfront revitalization will be an important economic growth and development tool for Green Bay   said Sen  Kohl   This project will help create jobs and promote economic development around the shoreline     The City of Green Bay is working on a waterfront revitalization plan that will spur economic development along the waterfront  The C  Reiss Coal Dock has been a viable economic concern for over 100 years in downtown Green Bay  and for over the past forty years  the Company and the city have been working on relocating the facility  Relocation is mutually beneficial as C  Reiss occupies forty acres of prime downtown waterfront property  yet the shipping distance from the mouth of the Fox River to the facility continues to be a competitive liability  The FY2006 funding bill for the Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  was passed by the Senate Subcommittee on Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary and HUD today and is expected to be approved by the full Appropriations Committee on Thursday
