KOHL DIRECTS  200 000 TO KENOSHA AFFODABLE HOUSING PROJECT                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    July 19  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL DIRECTS  200 000 TO KENOSHA AFFODABLE HOUSING PROJECT     WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that the Senate appropriations bill that funds the Department of Housing and Urban Development includes  200 000 for the Kenosha Department of City Development for an affordable housing project in the City s Columbus Neighborhood   Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified the projects as priorities for funding as part of the FY2006 federal budget   ANeighborhoods can be revitalized through affordable housing  creating greater community involvement and an investment in the neighborhood s prosperity   I m pleased that this federal investment in Kenosha s project will help bring those goals closer   Kohl said  The Department of City Development requested federal funding to assist in the acquisition  tenant relocation and demolition of blighted buildings in the City s Columbus Neighborhood  These buildings will be replaced with the construction of approximately 14 affordable single family homes in an effort to increase homeownership within a predominantly renter based neighborhood  The goals of assembling the blighted buildings and constructing the affordable single family homes are  to reduce residential density  improve property conditions  create new affordable homeownership opportunities and to increase the level of homeownership in the neighborhood  The FY2006 funding bill for the Department of Housing and Urban Development  HUD  was passed by the Senate Subcommittee on Transportation  Treasury  the Judiciary and HUD today and is expected to be approved by the full Appropriations Committee on Thursday
