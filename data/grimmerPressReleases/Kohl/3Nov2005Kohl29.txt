KOHL ANNOUNCES MILC PROGRAM EXTENDED BY THE SENATE                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    November 3  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL ANNOUNCES MILC PROGRAM EXTENDED BY THE SENATE     Washington  DC Today U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced that the Senate has agreed to extend the Milk Income Loss Contract  MILC  program for two years as part of the year end budget bill  The MILC program  which expired in September 2005  has provided  414 million since 2001 in support to Wisconsin dairy farmers struggling with plummeting milk prices   Today the Senate has taken a very important step toward delivering much needed relief to family dairy farmers in Wisconsin and other states   said Kohl   Relief that is targeted  fair and essential to dairy farmers throughout the U S  Twice before the Senate has voted to extend MILC program only to have those efforts blocked by the House leadership  I hope that the House will vote to extend this important program without delay and without any adverse adjustments   Kohl helped establish the MILC program as part of the 2002 Farm Bill to end regional fighting over federal milk pricing policies   The House of Representatives is expected to consider its budget bill next week   That will by followed by a joint Senate House conference to resolve differences between the House and Senate measures   When differenences between the two bills are resolved the measure can be sent to the President and signed into law
