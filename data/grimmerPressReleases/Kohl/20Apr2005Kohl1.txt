KOHL SECURES  470 MILLION FOR HUMANITARIAN FOOD ASSISTANCE AS PART OF EMERGENCY FUNDING BILL                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    April 20  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES  470 MILLION FOR HUMANITARIAN FOOD ASSISTANCE AS PART OF EMERGENCY FUNDING BILL     WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Herb Kohl s  D WI  proposal to devote  470 million to international humanitarian food assistance as part of the  80 6 billion emergency spending bill was passed by the Senate today  In the wake of the urgent need for food aid following the tsunami disaster  the U S  Agency for International Development  USAID  had to divert assistance from many existing crises in other parts of the world  Kohl s bipartisan amendment provides funding for ongoing USAID efforts in Sudan  Ethiopia  Bangladesh and Africa  The original FY05 Supplemental Appropriations Bill provided only  150 million  which was insufficient for USAID to meet its commitments    The tsunami tragedy provides a very graphic reminder of our obligation to be a part of international relief efforts  What concerns me now  however  is how we proceed after the television networks have scaled back their coverage   Kohl said   Enormous needs remain in the tsunami region  And existing food aid for other countries was shifted to address this crisis  some even while in transit  The dire need for this humanitarian assistance in other regions    and the U S  commitment to address that need    continues    Kohl s amendment will allow the U S  to meet the estimated emergency food requirements in areas that have been identified by USAID for assistance this year  This includes Sudan  due to the conflict in Darfur  Ethiopia  due to drought  Bangladesh  due to flooding  western Africa  due to locusts  and the Democratic Republic of Congo  Chad  Rwanda and Uganda due to refugees and fighting  The amendment restores funding to developmental food aid programs that were diverted to meet emergencies in other parts of the world  These programs are vital to long term food security in those countries  The amendment also restores funds to the Food for Progress Program that were diverted to provide immediate assistance to victims of the Indian Ocean Tsunami   Kohl s proposal is supported by the Coalition for Food Aid  a group of international relief agencies  Their members include the American Red Cross  CARE  Catholic Relief Services  and Save the Children  The amendment is also endorsed by the U S  Conference of Catholic Bishops
