KOHL ANNOUNCES DODGEVILLE POLICE DEPARTMENT WILL RECEIVE  25 000 TO ENHANCE SAFETY IN SCHOOLS                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    September 9  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL ANNOUNCES DODGEVILLE POLICE DEPARTMENT WILL RECEIVE  25 000 TO ENHANCE SAFETY IN SCHOOLS     WASHINGTON  D C   U S  Senator Herb Kohl today announced that the Dodgeville Police Department will receive  25 000 from the U S  Justice Department to improve security at schools and on school grounds  Today s grant comes from the Justice Department s Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS  program    Secure Our Schools    that helps fund efforts to enhance schools safety  The grants will provide up to 50 percent of the total cost for municipalities to install metal detectors  locks  lighting  and other equipment that can help deter crime  The grants can also be used to cover 50 percent of the cost of security assessments and training  Kohl  a member of the Senate Appropriations and Judiciary committees  has been a strong supporter of the COPS program               Every child deserves to attend a safe and secure school  Parents and children shouldn t have to worry about the security of their school   As we work to ensure safe schools and safe communities  the COPS program has been an effective ally for local law enforcement   Every dollar makes a difference  so we certainly welcome today s funding for Dodgeville   Kohl said  The COPS program  administered through the Department of Justice  was passed into law in 1994   The goal was to place 100 000 new officers on the beat by 2000    a goal that was realized ahead of time and under budget   Grant programs also include  matching funds for the hiring of new officers  assistance for purchasing new technologies and equipment that result in officers spending more time on the streets  and financing the training of former military personnel for local law enforcement
