KOHL DIRECTS  200 000 TO JUNEAU COUNTY BUSINESS INCUBATOR PROJECT                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    October 21  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL DIRECTS  200 000 TO JUNEAU COUNTY BUSINESS INCUBATOR PROJECT     WASHINGTON    U S  Senator Herb Kohl announced today that the Senate appropriations bill that funds the Department of Housing and Urban Development includes  200 000 for the Juneau County Economic Development Corporation s  JCEDC  proposed small business incubator in the Village of Necedah  Late last evening the Senate passed the Treasury  Transportation  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill by a vote of 93 to 1  Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified this project as a priority for funding    This ambitious project will help Wisconsin small businesses learn and develop the skills necessary to compete in the 21st century economy   Kohl said   I m pleased that we were able to bring federal support to the initiative   The JCEDC business incubator  slated to be located in an existing four hundred thousand square foot industrial building located south of the Village of Necedah  is design to address Juneau County s high unemployment rate   The center will provide a setting in which small business entrepreneurs can obtain support for creating a business and employees can learn the skills necessary to be productive members of the labor force   JCEDC projects that eight to ten new businesses will graduate from the facility per year   The Senate Treasury  Transportation  Housing and Urban Development appropriations bill now goes to a joint Senate House conference committee for approval before it can be signed into law by the President
