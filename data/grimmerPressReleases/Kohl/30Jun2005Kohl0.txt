SPONSOR BILL TO TOUGHEN FOOD SAFETY LAWS TO PROTECT CONSUMERS  Kevin s Law is based on E  Coli case in Mount Horeb  Wisconsin    WASHINGTON    U S  Senators Herb Kohl and Russ Feingold today sponsored the Meat and Poultry Pathogen Reduction and Enforcement Act  also known as  Kevin s Law   to strengthen existing food safety laws to prevent infections from contaminants like E  Coli and salmonella   The law is named for Kevin Kowalcyk of Mount Horeb  Wisconsin  who died from an E  Coli infection when he was two and a half years old   According to estimates from the Centers for Disease Control  76 million illnesses  325 000 hospitalizations  and 5 000 deaths occur each year in the United States from foodborne diseases  sadly  the majority of these fatal incidents involve children     Barbara Kowalcyk went through what no mother should have to go through  she watched in agony as the life faded out of her little boy   She has since become a thoughtful advocate for tougher food safety laws and worked with me on this bill   These diseases are preventable   We have technology and understanding to improve the safety of America s meat and poultry  and it is high time that we do it   Kohl said    Our food standards must be strengthened to protect Wisconsinites from tragedies like that of Kevin Kowalcyk   Feingold said    By passing Kevin s law  we can better protect against pathogens like E  Coli and do more to prevent any more of these devastating tragedies   Kevin s Law grants the U S  Department of Agriculture  USDA  authority to strengthen the regulatory structure for food safety   It includes key provisions that will allow the USDA to conduct scientific surveys to identify the foodborne pathogens that represent the largest threat to our public health and to set and update pathogen reduction standards to reduce the presence of these pathogens in meat and poultry   Kevin s Law is a bipartisan bill sponsored also by Sens  Tom Harkin  D IA  and Arlen Specter  R PA   Kohl is the ranking member of the Senate Agriculture Appropriations Subcommittee  which has jurisdiction over the USDA and Food and Drug Administration budgets
