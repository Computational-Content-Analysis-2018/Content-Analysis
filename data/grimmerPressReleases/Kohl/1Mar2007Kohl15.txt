KOHL QUESTIONS SECRETARY GUTIERREZ ON KEY BUSINESS COMPETITIVENESS PROGRAM                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    March 1  2007    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL QUESTIONS SECRETARY GUTIERREZ ON KEY BUSINESS COMPETITIVENESS PROGRAM President s FY2008 budget halves Manufacturing Extension Partnership funding by 56  for the second year in a row     WASHINGTON    Today  U S  Senator Herb Kohl criticized the Administration s proposed budget cuts for the successful Manufacturing Extension Partnership program  MEP  during a Senate Appropriations Committee hearing with Secretary of Commerce Carlos Gutierrez  The MEP program is a nationwide network of centers that helps small  and medium sized manufacturers compete in the global economy by producing advanced products  using emerging technology  and streamlined processes   The President s budget for FY2008 slashes funding for the MEP program by 56 percent   Kohl serves on the Appropriations Committee and its subcommittee with jurisdiction over the Department of Commerce budget   I know budgets are tight  but if you take  106 million and spread it over the MEP centers in the fifty states and Puerto Rico  you are talking about  2 million per state to help manufacturers stay competitive   Kohl said   Are you saying we can not afford to invest  2 million per state in a program that is helping manufacturers keep jobs in the United States   According to data released by the MEP program  their centers across the United States generated nearly  6 3 billion in sales   1 3 billion in cost savings   2 2 billion in investments  and created or retained over 53 000 jobs in 2006  Kohl added   This is the kind of program we should be investing more in  not less   How does the Administration plan to replace all the effective work of the MEP Centers with just a  46 million budget   Earlier this year  Kohl and Senator Olympia Snowe introduced a bill to fully fund the MEP program by authorizing  110 to  130 million each year in federal funding for MEP centers for the next five years  Wisconsin is home to two MEP centers  which both have a significant impact on the productivity of companies throughout the state   In Wisconsin  MEP has helped over 1 300 Wisconsin manufacturers make nearly  400 million in improvements in technology  productivity  and profits over the past decade   And  since 1994  the Northwest Wisconsin Manufacturing Outreach Center  targeting the more rural northwestern part of the State  has provided over 3 000 technical assistance activities to over 940 companies  creating or retaining almost 2 000 jobs    In Wisconsin  larger corporations like John Deere  Harley Davidson  and Oshkosh Truck are working with the state s MEP centers to develop domestic supply chains  On March 14  2006  the U S  Senate approved an amendment sponsored by Kohl and Snowe to boost funding for the MEP program to  106 million in the FY07 federal budget   The Senators also worked to ensure that the MEP program received  107 5 million in the FY2005 Omnibus Appropriations bill
