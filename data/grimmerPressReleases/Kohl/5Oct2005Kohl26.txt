KOHL   WE MUST ENSURE THAT THESE TRAGEDIES NEVER HAPPEN AGAIN                     Press Release      For Immediate Release    October 5  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL   WE MUST ENSURE THAT THESE TRAGEDIES NEVER HAPPEN AGAIN      WASHINGTON  D C     Senator Herb Kohl  lead Democrat of the U S  Senate Special Committee on Aging  today highlighted efforts underway in the Senate to prevent future catastrophes  like Hurricane Katrina  from leaving seniors and the disabled behind               Following disturbing reports from Louisiana on the abandonment of seniors and disabled individuals during the Hurricane Katrina disaster  Senator Herb Kohl  D WI   Ranking Member of the Senate Special Committee on Aging  last week asked Daniel Levinson  Inspector General of the Department of Health and Human Services  to immediately begin an investigation into the evacuation plans of health care facilities  including nursing homes  in the Hurricane Katrina affected areas of the Gulf Coast              Kohl said in the letter   Like many Americans  I was deeply saddened and outraged by the loss of life suffered during and in the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina   Countless seniors were abandoned in nursing homes  hospitals and care facilities  and we may never know how many were left in their own homes without any support   We must ensure that the tragedies we have witnessed in recent weeks never happen again      Under current statute  both federal and state law requires that health care facilities develop and maintain detailed plans that provide for safe and effective evacuation of patients  including hospitals and nursing homes              In the official request  Kohl asked the federal government to conduct a thorough investigation into where evacuation plans failed during and after Hurricane Katrina  the adequacy of current evacuation measures in place and plans to provide food  water  medication and temporary housing for rescued patients  the homebound and disabled
