KOHL ANNOUNCES EXPANSION OF FRESH FRUIT AND VEGETABLE PROGRAMS TO HELP WISCONSIN S CHILDREN                     Press Release      For Immediate Release    September 22  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL ANNOUNCES EXPANSION OF FRESH FRUIT AND VEGETABLE PROGRAMS TO HELP WISCONSIN S CHILDREN      Washington  D C     U S  Sen  Herb Kohl continued his fight for Wisconsin s children by announcing  expansion of the United States Department of Agriculture s  USDA  School Fruit and Vegetable Pilot Program  FVPP  to two states including Wisconsin  The program makes fresh fruit and vegetables available free of charge to students in eligible schools   This expansion is part of the FY2006 Agriculture Appropriations Bill which was approved by the Senate this morning  Kohl is the ranking member on the Senate Agriculture Appropriations Subcommittee  and a leading advocate of child nutrition programs  The FVPP is intended to increase a student s opportunities to eat fresh and dried fruit and fresh vegetables in the school environment outside traditional USDA meal programs   We need to teach the importance and value of nutritious eating in our schools   said Kohl   By expanding the Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Pilot Program  Wisconsin s children will have a chance to learn healthy eating habits that will benefit them their whole lives   The quality of children s diets is a subject of considerable public concern  as is the role of the school environment in promoting healthful eating habits  National food consumption survey data indicate that dietary intakes of U S  children and adolescents are less than optimal in several respects  Notably  the prevalence of obesity among youth has increased dramatically in recent years  suggesting that they are eating more calories than they need  At the same time  fruit and vegetable consumption among the FVPP target group is below recommended levels  Children ages 6 19 consumed on average half the recommended minimum of fruit servings in 1994 96   School staff believed that the pilot helped lessened the risk of obesity  increased attention in class  reduced consumption of less healthy food  reduced number of unhealthy snacks brought from home  In addition the School staff also found that the pilot  increased students  awareness and preference for a variety of fruits and vegetables  particularly less familiar kinds  such as kiwis and fresh pears   helped children who would otherwise be hungry get more food  and increased students  consumption of fruit and vegetables at lunch  The Senate Agriculture Appropriations bill now goes to a joint Senate House conference committee for approval before it can be signed into law by the President
