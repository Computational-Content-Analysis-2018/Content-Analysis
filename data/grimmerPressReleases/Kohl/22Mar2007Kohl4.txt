SENATE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE APPROVES ONE MONTH EXTENSION FOR KOHL S MILC PROGRAM                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    March 22  2007    Phone   202  224 5653        SENATE APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE APPROVES ONE MONTH EXTENSION FOR KOHL S MILC PROGRAM     WASHINGTON    The Senate Appropriations Committee today approved a one month extension of the Milk Income Loss Contract  MILC  program  from August to September of 2007  to coincide with the duration of other farm programs that will be considered as part of the new Farm Bill this year   The amendment  sponsored by Sens  Patrick Leahy  D VT  and Herb Kohl  D WI   will also build the cost of the dairy program into the baseline budget for the next Farm Bill   MILC is a countercyclical program that serves as a safety net to small and mid size dairy farmers when milk prices plummet   The program provided  414 million to Wisconsin dairy farmers when milk prices dropped to record lows since its implementation in 2002     We re encouraged that the Committee refused to allow the MILC program to terminate before it receives a full and fair debate as part of the new Farm Bill   Kohl said    We ll continue to work to build a bipartisan and regionally balanced alliance to support MILC and the invaluable safety net it provides for our dairy farmers and rural economies   Kohl helped establish the MILC program to end regional fighting over federal milk pricing policies and as an alternative to the Northeast Dairy Compact he helped defeat   The program was included in the 2002 Farm Bill and was designed to target small and mid size dairy operations   The MILC program faces stiff opposition from giant corporate producers in the West  The Administration s Farm Bill proposal calls for continuation of MILC  but with a reduced  level of support over time  and Kohl will be working to maintain a safety net for family sized dairy farms as the debate over the Farm Bill unfolds
