TWO COPS GRANTS BOOST LAW ENFORCEMENT EFFORTS IN MILWAUKEE  WEST ALLIS SCHOOLS                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    September 7  2006    Phone   202  224 5653        TWO COPS GRANTS BOOST LAW ENFORCEMENT EFFORTS IN MILWAUKEE  WEST ALLIS SCHOOLS     Washington  DC    U S  Senator Herb Kohl  D WI  announced today that two Wisconsin law enforcement offices have won important federal grants to help improve safety measures at Milwaukee and West Allis schools   The police departments in Milwaukee and West Allis will receive grants totaling  344 861 and  197 663  respectively  from the Justice Department s Community Oriented Policing Services  COPS   Secure Our Schools  program  The grants provide up to 50 percent of the funding for security assessments  training  and equipment utilized on school property    In order for kids to focus on their studies  our schools must be a sanctuary for them   said Kohl   At many schools  we count on one officer to keep a thousand students safe  These grants will help give them the right tools and training they need to shoulder this weighty responsibility   Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and the Subcommittee that funds the Department of Justice  and has been a strong supporter of the law enforcement assistance program  The COPS program  administered through the Department of Justice  was passed into law in 1994   The goal was to place 100 000 new officers on the beat by 2000    a goal that was realized ahead of time and under budget   Grant programs also include  matching funds for the hiring of new officers  assistance for purchasing new technologies and equipment that result in officers spending more time on the streets  and financing the training of former military personnel for local law enforcement
