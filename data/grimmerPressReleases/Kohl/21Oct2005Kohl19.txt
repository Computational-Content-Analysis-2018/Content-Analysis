KOHL SECURES FOR FUNDING FOR HIGH SPEED RAIL INITIATIVE  WISCONSIN BUS SYSTEM                    Press Release      For Immediate Release    October 21  2005    Phone   202  224 5653        KOHL SECURES FOR FUNDING FOR HIGH SPEED RAIL INITIATIVE  WISCONSIN BUS SYSTEM      WASHINGTON U S   Senator Herb Kohl announced today that he secured  500 000 in federal funding for the Midwest Regional Rail Initiative and  10 million for Wisconsin bus capital as part of the FY 2006 budget for the Department of Transportation  Late last evening the Senate passed the Transportation  Treasury  Housing and Urban Development Appropriations bill by a vote of 93 to 1  Kohl is a member of the Senate Appropriations Committee and had identified these projects as priorities for funding    A strong federal investment in rail and transit is critical to the thousands of travelers in Wisconsin who rely on these services   Kohl said   A safe and efficient transportation network directly contributes to the State s economic growth and development    these two projects will help bring tourists and business travelers into the State and will help Wisconsinites get to work every day   Kohl included  500 000 for the Midwest Regional Rail Initiative  a collaborative effort among several states to bring high speed rail to the Midwest   The funding will be used to study the feasibility of high speed rail in the Milwaukee to Chicago corridor   Ultimately  the Initiative aims to develop a passenger rail system that will offer shorter travel times for commuters between Milwaukee and Chicago  resulting in increased ridership and reduced congestion on roadways in the area   Kohl also included  10 million for the Wisconsin Department of Transportation for costs associated with transit systems throughout the state   Wisconsin s transit systems rely on an aging bus fleet in need of replacement   This funding will help ensure that necessary maintenance and updates are completed  increasing passenger safety as well as operating efficiency and effectiveness  In addition  funding would be used for the construction of transit related facilities throughout Wisconsin   The Senate Transportation  Treasury  Transportation  Housing and Urban Development appropriations bill now goes to a joint Senate House conference committee for approval before it can be signed into law by the President
