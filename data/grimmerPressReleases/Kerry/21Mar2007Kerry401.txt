       Releases                                                                                                                                           03 21 2007 Kerry  Kennedy  Delahunt announce Cape Cod National Seashore to receive  2 million for land preservation                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               BOSTON   Senators John Kerry and Edward Kennedy  along with Congressman William Delahunt today announced that the Cape Cod National Seashore will receive  2 million to purchase and preserve additional land along Massachusetts  shore         This funding will help to further protect the Cape s natural beauty from development and will preserve its natural resources for future generations to enjoy   said Senator Kerry   I am pleased to have worked with Senator Kennedy and Congressman Delahunt  as well as with the Trust for Public Land to ensure that this part of our state will remain a national treasure for years to come          The Cape Cod National Seashore is one of our state s most treasured natural resources  and I thoroughly enjoyed our visit last fall   I m grateful that this funding is coming through   Senator Kennedy said         I have found this campground to be one of the most pristine and scenic areas in the entire park   Delahunt said today   I have been a strong supporter of this acquisition effort because all throughout our region  campers and those who love the outdoors are loosing access to campsites like this where families can visit and vacation together   Our success to date is also a tribute to the efforts of the Seashore  the Trust for Public Lands and local residents          We are very grateful to Senators Kennedy and Kerry and Representative Delahunt for working with the National Park Service and the Cape Cod National Seashore Park Advisory Committee to successfully secure FY 2007 funding to protect critical recreational land on Cape Cod   said Craig MacDonnell  The Trust for Public Land s Massachusetts State Director    Future generations of Bay Staters will benefit greatly from their efforts  and we applaud their leadership on improving the quality of the natural environment on Cape Cod  and specifically on behalf of the National Seashore         The  2 million from the Land and Water Conservation Fund will be used by the National Park Service for the initial phase of the North of Highland Campground to ensure recreational opportunities for the public         With over four million visitors a year  the Cape Cod National Seashore is one of the most heavily visited national parks in the country  The seashore includes 40 miles of coastline and boasts some of the world s most beautiful white sand beaches  It covers a significant portion of the northern tip of the Cape Cod peninsula   a landscape that contains numerous natural resources and historic structures
