       Releases                                                                                                                                           04 16 2007 Kerry Applauds Coast Guard s Decision to Use Open Competition on Deepwater Contract                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    WASHINGTON D C    Today  Senator John Kerry  D Mass   made the following statement on the decision by the Coast Guard to take over management of the Deepwater Contract and modify the Northrop   Lockheed contract to ensure a full and open competitive bidding process for future acquisitions           The Deepwater Program has been plagued with skyrocketing costs and poor oversight for months   Kerry introduced the Deepwater Accountability Act in March which called for the Coast Guard to make these changes          The Coast Guard has made a very smart decision to drop Lockheed Martin and Northrop Grumman as project managers and use a competitive bidding process for future Deepwater assets   Senator Kerry said   For too long  essential national security projects have been stalled and millions of American taxpayer dollars have gone down the drain because of mismanagement and waste in this contract   The Coast Guard has read the writing on the wall and made the right choice to move Deepwater forward   I m confident the Coast Guard will sign the best contract possible in June  and I look forward to working with them to ensure the same mistakes aren t made twice
