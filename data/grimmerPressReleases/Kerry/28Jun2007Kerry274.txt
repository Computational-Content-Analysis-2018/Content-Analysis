       Releases                                                                                                                                           06 28 2007 Kerry announces Fall River and Brockton to receive funding to help at risk youth prepare for future                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    BOSTON  Senator John Kerry today announced that the cities of Fall River and Brockton will receive  491 666 each as part of the Department of Labors Multiple Education Pathways Planning Grants      The grants aim to serve as a catalyst between at risk youth and community partners  to ensure that young people in Massachusetts are prepared for 21st Century careers  The funds will help to build and implement programs that connect young high school dropouts to alternative learning opportunities  The projects will prepare young people for careers and to pursue post secondary education       This funding will enable many Massachusetts young people to have a second chance at the American dream  From preparing for jobs in the 21st Century workplace   to recognizing various educational pathways  the possibilities that come with such a program are invaluable  said Senator Kerry  I congratulate both Fall River and Brockton for receiving this funding and I commend the Department of Labor for investing in the youth of Massachusetts     Nationwide  only four other cities  population 70 000 350 000  received the grant                                                                                  20510
