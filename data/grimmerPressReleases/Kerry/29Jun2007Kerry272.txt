       Releases                                                                                                                                           06 29 2007 Kerry on Marty Meehan s last day in Congress                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           BOSTON  Senator John Kerry today issued the following statement today on Marty Meehans last day in Congress     For 14 years  Marty Meehan has represented the 5th District of Massachusetts with honor  vision and courage  His trip from the Sacred Heart neighborhood in Lowell to the Chancellors office was not exactly the shortest distance between two points  and for that we should all be grateful      Marty never let serving in the Congress become more important to him than his constituents  He worked alongside Chris Shays of Connecticut to rewrite campaign finance law to help make politicians more accountable to the people who elect them  Like Paul Tsongas before him  he was a deficit hawk who believed in defense spending before either of those became popular Democratic positions  And in 1994  he wrote a visionary memo that grew into the lawsuits against Big Tobacco      All of us owe a great debt to Marty  In Congress we will miss his investigative skill and his maverick streak  And Massachusetts will surely also miss his role as a protector of our military bases on the Armed Services Committee  UMASS Lowell is lucky to be getting a leader like Marty at the helm       I will miss his insights and gutsy stands  but Im proud of him and I wish him well on a new chapter in an already distinguished and remarkable career
