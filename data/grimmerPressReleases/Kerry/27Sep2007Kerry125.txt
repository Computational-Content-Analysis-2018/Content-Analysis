       Releases                                                                                                                                           09 27 2007 Kerry fights to improve safety of railroad bridges                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     Washington  DC  Senator John Kerry today joined colleague   D MN  to secure improvements to bridge safety for rail traffic  Their amendment was attached to the Railroad Safety Enhancement Act  which passed out of the Commerce Committee today     Every year millions of Americans depend on trains to get where they need to go  but we have miles to go to make our nations rail system and its bridges safe enough  said Senator Kerry  In the last eight years 22 train accidents were caused by bridge structural failures  This legislation will require the inspection and upkeep of all railroad bridges across Massachusetts and the rest of the country     Kerry and Klobuchars amendment adds to the list of goals for the bills five year strategy improving the safety of railroad bridges and related infrastructure to prevent accidents  injuries  and fatalities caused by catastrophic failures and other bridge failures  The amendment would also ensure that new inspectors be required to inspect railroad bridges and related infrastructure  and to review or analyze railroad bridge and related infrastructure inspection reports     The original bill requires the Secretary of Transportation to develop a five year strategy for improving rail safety  It also adds 200 safety inspectors at the Federal Rail Administration between 2008 and 2013                                                                          20510
