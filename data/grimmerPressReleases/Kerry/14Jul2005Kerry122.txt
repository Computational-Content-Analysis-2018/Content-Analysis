       Releases                                                                                                                                           07 14 2005 Statement of Senator John Kerry on Homeland Security Bill                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 What   s most shocking about this homeland security debate is that we   re again and again robbing Peter to pay Paul   In a time of war we refuse to touch tax cuts for the wealthiest Americans in order to meet every homeland security need  and the Senate is cutting funding for security on mass transit in this homeland security bill   The Republicans who run Washington are turning the idea of sacrifice on its head         That said  until we have a different Congress and a different Administration  we are where we are     we   re in the business of either making tough choices or making political choices   I   d rather make tough choices and let the chips fall where they may   We have to be smart and strategic about how we invest homeland security dollars  and that means making honest decisions that direct money to the highest risk population centers and other high profile targets   We need to allocate money based on risk  not on some bureaucratic formula that doesn   t take into account the riskiness of targets   It   s just makes sense given what we know about 9 11 and the recent attacks in London   Terrorists aren   t operating in some sort of bureaucratic la la land  and neither should we
