       Releases                                                                                                                                           11 22 2006 Danvers                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               The day before Thanksgiving  a massive explosion at CAI  Inc  in Danvers displaced over 200 residents and damaged more than 70 homes  The afternoon of the explosion  Senator Kerry  along with Congressman Tierney and State Senator Fred Berry  visited the site of the blast  They toured the site in order to view the damage and met with State Fire Marshall Stephen Coan  Fire Chief James Tutko  Town Manager Wayne Marquis and many of the first responders  As Danvers residents began to return to their homes  Senator Kerry  Senator Kennedy and Congressman Tierney announced that the Small Business Administration  SBA  issued physical and economic injury disaster declarations for the town of Danvers  making low interest federal loans available to victims of the explosion  Senator Kerry said  I am pleased that the SBA has responded to the needs of the residents of Danvers and to the requests of our congressional delegation  Because of these disaster declarations  victims of last weeks explosion will be able to obtain low interest loans to rebuild their homes  their businesses and their lives  Im proud to have worked with my colleagues  Senator Kennedy and Congressman Tierney  in securing this much needed assistance from the SBA  As incoming Chair of the Senate Committee on Small Business and Entrepreneurship  I will continue to work with the SBA and with state and local officials to ensure that Danvers can get back to business                                                                          20510
