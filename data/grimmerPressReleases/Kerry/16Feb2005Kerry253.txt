       Releases                                                                                                                                           02 16 2005 John Kerry on Climate Change                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      It s time for America to re engage with the rest of the world on this very real problem                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                Today the United States doesn t have a seat at the table when it comes to the global environment   We cannot wait for Kyoto to expire to consider next steps   We need to signal to the world that we are prepared to shoulder our fair share  And we need to put action behind our words by accepting responsible pollution limits and engaging the developing world   Their emissions may be a fraction of ours now  but without action they will soon exceed the largest nations of the developed world            The Kyoto protocol goes into effect today while our government refuses to adopt an active approach or even work with the international community to find alternative  acceptable solutions  While the Bush administration stonewalls  others are moving forward   It shows that our allies are prepared to set the global agenda with or without the United States  and that is neither good environmental policy nor good for our own best interests            Climate change is real   The science is compelling   And the longer we wait  the harder the problem will be to solve           Those of us who want to act  not just talk  about the dangers of climate change must take our message out to Main Street America   Unlike Washington  which is stuck in ideological gridlock  Americans feel the impact of climate change in their own hometowns and they know something must be done            In Arizona and Nevada  I met with officials trying to find solutions to the dwindling water supply after the West suffered through its fifth straight year of drought   In Ohio  I met hunters who have seen the birds change their migratory patterns with warming temperatures   The American people understand we cannot pollute the environment without consequence          It   s time for America to re engage with the rest of the world on this very real problem   Addressing climate change is not just a matter of the environment   It   s a matter of public health  economic growth and our own security
