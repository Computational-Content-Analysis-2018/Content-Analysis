       Releases                                                                                                                                           07 26 2007 Kerry  Kennedy  McGovern and Frank Announce Quaker Fabric Employees to Receive Federal Assistance                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      BOSTON  Senators John Kerry and Edward Kennedy  along with Congressmen James McGovern and Barney Frank announced that workers affected by the closing of Quaker Fabric are eligible to receive financial assistance under the Trade Adjustment Assistance  TAA  program  TAA provides training for American workers who have lost their jobs to foreign competitors so that they can transition into new industries  The Fall River company was recently forced to close its doors and lay off 900 employees due to competition from foreign textile manufacturers        I am thrilled that the hundreds of workers who lost their jobs at Quaker Fabric will receive financial assistance  This funding will help them to transition into other industries and continue to contribute to Fall Rivers economy  said Senator Kerry  These are hardworking people who have played by the rules and I am happy that we could help them through these difficult economic times  I commend the Department of Labor for its quick turnaround  and I hope it acts just as quickly on approving the states National Emergency Grant request       Senator Kennedy said  Im pleased that Department of Labor has recognized the urgent needs of the hard working employees of Quaker Fabrics  This needed assistance is a first step in ensuring that these families receive the benefits and training they deserve        Im very pleased that the Department of Labor has moved quickly to approve this federal trade assistance  Its essential that the workers at Quaker Fabric get the help they need  and I will continue to work with my colleagues to find every available federal resource  said Congressman McGovern       I am pleased that these important benefits will be made available to Quaker employees  and I will continue working with my colleagues in Congress to push for any additional assistance the Federal Government can provide  said Congressman Frank       The TAA program is a federal program established that provides aid to workers who lose their jobs or whose hours of work and wages are reduced as a result of increased imports  Workers may be eligible for training  job search and relocation allowances  income support and other reemployment services
