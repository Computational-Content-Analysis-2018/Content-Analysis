       Releases                                                                                                                                           09 27 2007 Kerry  Kennedy announce Massachusetts to receive more than  3 million from USDA for excellence in administering food stamps                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            BOSTON   Senators John Kerry and Edward Kennedy today announced that Massachusetts will receive  3 016 937 from the USDA on account of its exceptional administration of food stamps  The funding is part of two awards    1 726 484 for most improved program access and  1 290 453 for best application processing timeliness rate     The administrators of our states food stamp program should be commended for their commitment  dedication to improvement  and significant contribution to improving health and well being of people in Massachusetts  said Senator Kerry  Food stamps provide a lifeline for our states neediest and most vulnerable citizens  This funding recognizes the outstanding effort of our states program and will help alleviate hunger in Massachusetts     Feeding the hungry is a basic priority  I m delighted that Massachusetts has done so much to improve its outreach to those eligible for food stamps and is leading the nation in promptly processing the applications it receives  said Senator Kennedy     The awards are authorized by the 2002 Farm Bill  which grants  12 million in high performance bonuses among the eight States that have excelled in the area of program access  Another  6 million was awarded to the six States that processed Food Stamp applications in the timeliest manner  In total  14 states received bonuses  totaling  18 million
