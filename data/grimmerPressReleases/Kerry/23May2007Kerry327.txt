       Releases                                                                                                                                           05 23 2007 Kerry  An Iraq Bill Without a Deadline is Meaningless                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             Says Timeframe for Redeployment of Troops is Only Way Forward                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       WASHINGTON  D C    Sen  John Kerry issued the following statement today on his plan to vote  no  on a military spending bill that no longer contains any binding deadline for the redeployment of troops from Iraq         We support the troops by getting the policy right and this bill allows the President to keep getting the policy wrong  We need a deadline to force Iraqis to stand up for Iraq and bring our heroes home  not watered down benchmarks and blank check waivers for this President  We support the troops by funding the right mission  not with a White House that opposes a pay raise for our brave men and women in uniform   Kerry said   The original Senate legislation offered a roadmap to change course in Iraq  This new version enables the Administration and Iraqi politicians to deliver more of the same  I am determined to continue pressing this issue until President Bush changes course  We owe our troops nothing less than a strategy that is worthy of their sacrifice
