       Releases                                                                                                                                           02 08 2007 Kerry  Climate Change Vote Essential Step Forward                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     WASHINGTON  D C    Senator John Kerry  D Mass   today made the following statement on his amendment  which would have directed the Army Corps of Engineers to factor climate change into all future water projects   The text of the bill can be found    The amendment   the first vote on climate change of the 110th Congress   won support from a majority of Senators but failed in a 51 42 vote  The amendment needed 60 votes to pass         By having this vote  we took an essential step forward in our battle to make Congress address global climate change   Kerry said   Tonight we got a majority of Senators to stand up and demand that climate change be taken seriously  We will keep growing this majority until we succeed  We ve waited long enough   too many in Congress have turned a blind eye to this looming crisis and avoided a real discussion on its effects  Tonight we said  enough is enough   With this vote  the United States Senate has gone on record about global warming  and sent a statement that its impact must be considered in our public policy debates  I thank the co sponsors of this amendment and look forward to our joint efforts in advancing our urgent climate change goals
