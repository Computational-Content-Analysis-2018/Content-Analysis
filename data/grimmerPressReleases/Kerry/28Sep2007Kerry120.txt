       Releases                                                                                                                                           09 28 2007 Kerry  Kennedy announce Emergency Response Preparedness Program at BU to receive  2 4 million                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          BOSTON  Senators John Kerry and Edward Kennedy today announced that the Partnership for Effective Emergency Response  PEER  in Boston will receive  2 4 million in funding from the Department of Health and Human Services  HHS     PEER is part of Boston Universitys School of Public Health  The school will use the funding for medical response exercise planning and implementation throughout the region     Both Kerry and Kennedy wrote to HHS over the summer urging it to make these funds available    From the shores of Cape Cod to the hills of the Berkshires  every community in Massachusetts should be able to count on effective communication to help in emergency response situations  said Senator Kerry  This critical funding will permit PEER to improve preparedness throughout our state so that lives are saved in the event of an emergency    It s essential that we do more to strengthen our health emergency response centers to deal with sudden outbreaks of disease or other health crises  said Senator Kennedy  These federal funds will enable BU s PEER program to continue its indispensable planning to help our communities to be as well prepared as possible for any future emergencies in any part of our Commonwealth    The partnership represents 62 cities and towns throughout Massachusetts                                                                          20510
