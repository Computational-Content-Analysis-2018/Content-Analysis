       Releases                                                                                                                                           12 20 2007 Kerry  Kennedy  McGovern Announce Central and Southeastern Massachusetts to receive more than  7 million in federal funding                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Massachusetts communities statewide will see more than  236 million in investments                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  BOSTON  Senators John Kerry and Edward Kennedy  with Congressman James McGovern  today announced that the omnibus appropriations bill approved by Congress contains  7 681 000 in federal assistance for parts of central and southeastern Massachusetts  Statewide  Massachusetts initiatives received a total of more than  236 million  The Consolidated Appropriations Act  2008 will fund 15 cabinet agencies and other federal agencies for Fiscal Year 2008  It includes the following appropriations bills  Agriculture  Commerce Justice State  Energy Water  Foreign Operations  Interior  Labor Health and Human Services Education  Legislative Branch  Transportation Treasury  and Veterans Administration Housing and Urban Development  The bill now goes to the Presidents desk for his signature  This is a victory for crucial economic development projects throughout the region  From transportation to health care to social services to education  this money will help make a difference in the quality of life of those who live and work in central and southeastern Massachusetts  said Senator Kerry  By making these investments in our communities  we are investing in our states economic future  and that is why Senator Kennedy and Congresswoman McGovern and I fight each year to secure federal funding for these initiatives that make a real local impact  In Massachusetts  we re enormously proud of our world class hospitals  research centers and universities  They lead the nation and the world in improving the lives of our citizens  said Senator Kennedy  These federal investments are vital to the people of central and southeastern Massachusetts  and our whole Commonwealth  so that Massachusetts will stay at the forefront of the global economy  and future generations can reach their full potential  Im very pleased that Senator Kerry  Senator Kennedy and I have been able to secure this important new federal funding  Rep  McGovern said  These are important priorities for our community  economic development  education  health care  environmental clean up  transportation and homeland security  The federal government can and should be a partner in these priorities  The Massachusetts College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences  MCPHS  is one of the projects set to receiving funding  Since 2000  Massachusetts College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences has been committed to helping revitalize Worcester by bringing students and highly trained faculty to the Downtown area  said MCPHS President Charles F  Monahan  a Worcester native  Thanks to the hard work of Congressman McGovern and Senators Kerry and Kennedy  this funding will help us continue to expand our health science programs and technological infrastructure  Federal assistance in the omnibus bill for central and southeastern Massachusetts includes  Massachusetts Projects in the FY 2008 Energy and Water Appropriations Conference Report   200 000 for Blackstone River Watershed Restoration    2 million for the conductive  transplant Coating Solar Cell project Massachusetts Projects in the FY 2008 Interior Appropriations Conference Report   300 000 for the City of Marlborough Wastewater Treatment program   500 000 for the Fall River  New Bedford wastewater treatment program Massachusetts Projects in the FY 2008 Labor HHS Appropriations Conference Report   82 000 for the Molly Bish Center Anna Maria College  for program development for the protection of Children and the Elderly    195 000 for Dean College  to develop programs and procure equipment for the Learning Center    121 000 for Assumption College  for program development  including equipment    165 000 for Bristol Community College  to expand adult literacy and career development academic programs    877 000 for UMASS Memorial Hospital in Worcester  for health information technology    341 000 for the Mass College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences in Worcester  for health information technology systems  Massachusetts Projects in the
