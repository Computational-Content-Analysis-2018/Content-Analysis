       Releases                                                                                                                                           07 16 2007 Kerry Amendment Boosts SCHIP to  50 Billion                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            WASHINGTON D C   Senator John Kerry announced today that he will offer an amendment during the mark up in the Senate Finance Committee of the State Childrens Health Insurance Program  S CHIP  that will increase funding of the program to  50 billion over 5 years   Last week  members of the Senate Finance Committee agreed to set aside  35 billion dollars to SCHIP over 5 years   15 billion dollars less than that allotted in the budget passed this winter  The Kerry amendment will boost funding to the level approved in the budget     50 billion over 5 years  to cover approximately 4 5 million uninsured children   This past Friday  President Bush threatened to veto funding for S CHIP   We have a responsibility to fight for childrens health insurance  Senator Kerry said  With more than 9 million kids living without health care in this country and a Republican President who has vowed to veto S CHIP funding  this isnt the time to retreat from the fight  This week we must move forward with a bold solution to tackle this problem and fund the program to  50 billion dollars  I am looking forward to the debate on this issue and hope I can convince a majority of my colleagues to support this common sense measure
