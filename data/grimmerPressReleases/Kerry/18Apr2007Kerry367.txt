quality at Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   BOSTON   Senator John Kerry today said that the habitat quality at Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary on Cape Cod is  unacceptable    The senator s statement follows the release of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration s National  NOAA  Condition Report 2007 that highlights degraded conditions of key habitats and living resources in the sanctuary         The NOAA report needs to be a wake up call   Fifteen years ago  we designated Stellwagen as a marine sanctuary to preserve it for future generations   said Senator Kerry   These latest findings are unacceptable  We must take action now in order to address the problems in the sanctuary so we can get back on the path towards preserving the marine and ecology health of Massachusetts Bay         Senator Kerry has consistently fought for commonsense policies to improve the overall condition of Stellwagen Bank including increased funding to battle water pollution and additional protections for marine mammals from ship strikes and fishing gear entanglement         The NOAA report says that over half the categories  10 of 17  they use to rate habitat quality had fair through poor ratings   The report also cites bottom dwelling and particularly marine mammals as specifically at risk                                                                                    20510
