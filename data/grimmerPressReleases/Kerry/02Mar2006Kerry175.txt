       Releases                                                                                                                                           03 02 2006 John Kerry on Patriot Act Vote in the Senate                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      Below is a statement from Senator John Kerry in the Senate   s vote today on the PATRIOT Act                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             We have been telling the White House all along that we can strike the right balance between protecting our national security and protecting our rights in a time of war  and these changes to the original PATRIOT Act are a big step forward         The changes the Senate made today address some of my key concerns   The law ensures regular public libraries will not have to disclose patrons    records          I think some protections for citizens should go even further   I   m committed to continue working to improve the PATRIOT Act   I think people have a right to know within a week  rather than a month or two  if the government secretly entered their home and searched through their things          After a long fight  we were able to make some good  common sense changes to the PATRIOT Act that will protect our rights and our security                                                                                          20510
