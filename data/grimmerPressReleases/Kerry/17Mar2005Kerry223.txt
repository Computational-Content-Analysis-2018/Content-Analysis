       Releases                                                                                                                                           03 17 2005 John Kerry Statement on Senate Action to Save Medicaid from Drastic Cuts                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          Senator John Kerry s statement on passage of the Smith Bingaman Amendment to strike the president   s proposal to cut  15 billion in Medicaid funding                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    We won an important victory today for Americans who need a voice in Washington   In a bipartisan vote  the Senate stuck up for the poor children  pregnant women  seniors and people with disabilities who are served by Medicaid            President Bush   s budget chose to give millionaires a little more play money instead of living up to our responsibility to those in need  and the Senate correctly set our budget priorities straight           Thanks to Sen  Bingaman   s work with both Democrats and Republicans  we have made a bad budget a little bit more compassionate and consistent with bedrock American values   Now we need to work together not just to stop deep health care cuts from happening  but to address America s real and present crisis in health care for our children            Senator Kerry is a cosponsor of the Smith Bingaman amendment as well as a cosponsor of legislation they have introduced  S  338  to create a bipartisan Commission on Medicaid that would make recommendations about the long term financing and policy needs of the program   Today s amendment not only stripped the proposed funding cuts but provided for the establishment of the Commission
