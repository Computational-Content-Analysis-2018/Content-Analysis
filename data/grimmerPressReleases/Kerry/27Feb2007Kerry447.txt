       Releases                                                                                                                                           02 27 2007 Kerry Homeland Security Amendment Would Help Hospitals Communicate in a Disaster                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  Plan Would Also Direct FCC to Study TV Phone Backup                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 WASHINGTON  DC   Sen  John Kerry today announced a plan to provide hospitals with at least  20 million in grants to study ways to improve communications during a terrorist attack or natural disaster  The grant funds are aimed at preventing a repeat of the situation in New Orleans  when patients died as Hurricane Katrina essentially cut off Charity Hospital from the outside world for three days  Kerry also announced a plan to direct the Federal Communications Commission  FCC  to evaluate the feasibility of a backup system for emergency communications in case a disaster cuts off all television  radio and electric power  Senator Kerry s provisions are included in Commerce Committee s portion of the bill  of which he is a member  to include the provisions on the broader Homeland Security Bill   which was brought to the Senate floor this week        We know that we have a long way to go to prepare our country for another emergency   said Sen  Kerry   Helping our hospitals develop plans to cope during an emergency makes good sense and will ultimately benefit patients and families  Moreover  we need the FCC to think creatively about how to foster communication during a crisis  I believe that we should be willing to task modern technology to serve this role during an emergency  We must always be vigilant and prepared for another disaster because we never know when the next 9 11 or Katrina will come  I want to thank the Chairman and ranking member of the Commerce Committee for agreeing to these proposals                                                                                    20510
