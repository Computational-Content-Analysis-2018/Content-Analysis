       Releases                                                                                                                                           03 02 2007 Conversation on Crime                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 Senator Kerry convened a meeting of local  state and federal officials to have a Conversation on Crime at Suffolk University Law School  The participants  including Lieutenant Governor Tim Murray and Attorney General Martha Coakley  discussed the root causes of crime among juveniles and the steps needed to provide increased resources for education and crime prevention in communities across Massachusetts  We are feeling the affects of Americas crime epidemic right here in Massachusetts  But I believe we can help fix this tragic problem  Fridays meeting was a great first step  but much  much more needs to be done  We owe it to the thousands of victims of crime in this state to do that work  said Senator Kerry  Senator Kerry then dropped by the Museum of AfroAmerican Historys bicentennial celebration at the Four Seasons Hotel  There  he presented a proclamation to the museums Executive Director  Beverly MorganWelch  The gala honored Living Legends including Earl Graves  Glendora Putnam  and Kenneth Gamble  The museum is dedicated to preserving  conserving and accurately interpreting the contributions of African Americans in New England from the colonial period through the 19th century  Finally  the Senator ended the day by attending the farewell ceremony for the USS John F  Kennedy  Senator Kerry said  For nearly 40 years  the U S S  John F  Kennedy and her brave sailors have protected our nation  Now  as she visits Boston for the last time  its only fitting that we pause to honor her distinguished service  I am proud to be a part of the festivities to pay tribute to her and to  with the rest of Boston  wish her farewell                                                                          20510
